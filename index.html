Why this project

Supporting volunteers before, during, and after they say yes

This recreated toolkit was created for a Volunteer Coordinator application package. It is not an official county or shelter program and does not use confidential or employer-owned content. The goal is to show how my training, communication, engagement, and animal-welfare experience can translate into practical volunteer coordination support.

Honest framing: These are sample portfolio artifacts. They demonstrate transferable strengths in onboarding communication, volunteer recognition, recruitment, public-facing education, reporting mindset, and animal-welfare storytelling.

The coordinator challenge

Volunteer programs need clarity, confidence, and connection

Volunteers are more likely to stay engaged when they understand expectations, feel welcomed, see the impact of their work, and receive consistent communication. In a busy shelter environment, coordinator materials should help reduce confusion, make next steps easy, and create meaningful touchpoints for appreciation.

This toolkit focuses on four practical needs: recruiting prospective volunteers, educating the public about field trips, recognizing volunteers as whole people, and keeping volunteers connected through updates and impact storytelling.

Toolkit artifacts

Four sample pieces built around the volunteer experience

Field trip education and adoption storytelling

Tails Around Town Field Trip Flyer

Purpose: A public-facing flyer designed to explain the value of dog day trips, reduce common concerns, and help interested community members understand how outings support shelter dogs.

Role alignment: Supports public dog field trips, participant education, adoption visibility, community outreach, and recordkeeping through photos, videos, and behavior notes.

What it demonstrates: Translating a shelter program into clear, welcoming public education that encourages participation while setting realistic expectations.

Recognition, retention, and volunteer storytelling

Volunteer Recognition Spotlight

Purpose: A recognition feature designed to celebrate volunteers as whole people, not just names on a milestone list.

Role alignment: Supports volunteer retention, recognition programming, communication, engagement, and relationship-building.

What it demonstrates: Creating recognition that feels personal, scalable, and mission-connected by highlighting service, hours, favorite roles, motivation, and memorable shelter moments.

Recruitment, outreach, and expectation-setting

Volunteer Interest Open House Flyer

Purpose: A recruitment flyer for prospective volunteers who want to learn about shelter roles before applying.

Role alignment: Supports volunteer recruitment, community outreach, group inquiries, onboarding expectations, and education around application and background-check steps.

What it demonstrates: Creating recruitment materials that are welcoming but realistic, helping people understand the process before committing.

Communication, reminders, and impact updates

Paws & Connect Volunteer Newsletter

Purpose: A recurring communication piece for updates, reminders, recognition, impact metrics, and support needs.

Role alignment: Supports volunteer communication by email/newsletter, recognition, retention, reporting, scheduling reminders, and volunteer engagement.

What it demonstrates: Keeping volunteers informed and connected through consistent, organized, mission-centered communication.
